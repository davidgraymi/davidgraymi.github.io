Philosophy

As a developing computer scientist my philosophy revolves around the pursuit of knowledge, advancement in science, and improving the human experience. From a young age I have been fascinated by my own thoughts. Through the years, and with the right teachers, my fascination has evolved into a passion fueled search for understanding the complexities of human minds. This interest paired with my natural ability to excel in the sciences, particularly with computers, has led me to thinking deeply about human’s interaction with technology from a variety of perspectives. From an ethical perspective I see the advancement in technology and cannot deny the individual the right to benefit physically, mentally, or emotionally. There are many worries about artificial intelligence and integration of computers with people, but my job is not to decide on restrictions and limitations. My job is to develop, engineer, and innovate. From a technical perspective I group technology into three categories that include direct external technology, indirect external technology, and internal technology. Direct external technology within the scope of human-technology interaction is a physical interaction outside of human bodies such as a website layout. Indirect external technology can be defined as all technology that people do not encounter that makes a tangible end product possible. For example, the equipment that makes chemical reactions possible on a large scale so that companies can produce pharmaceuticals. Internal technology is physically in the body of a person. A good example is a pacemaker. As seen from my previous statements I have a people first approach to life.
